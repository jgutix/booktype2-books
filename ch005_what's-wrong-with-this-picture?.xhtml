but i'm also sure that you're quite aware of the problem areas.
indeed, given your experience, your appreciation of them is
probably even more keen than those of an outsider like myself.


and some of the commentators over at the o'reilly blog have
already mentioned some of the obvious concerns, although
i'm not sure they've pinned them done as good as they could.


but a large part of the equation involves the question of focus.


my focus is on books for which the single authorial voice is
a very important factor -- perhaps even the _most_ crucial one.
so, at least for the _writing_ part, collaboration isn't even desired.
and even in terms of _feedback,_ it's hard to find the right level.
my experience is that authors either get far too much feedback
-- more than they can possibly use, especially since it's mixed --
or (as is more often the case) not much valuable feedback at all.


moreover, that's the kind of book for which it is important to
have the thing be a coherent and stable work, with integrity.
because without that, it's impossible to get a handle on it,
so you can formulate thoughts about it, and discuss them.


on the other hand, there are other types of books which have
nearly the opposite demands, and manuals are among them.
for books like these, there is no need for an "authorial" voice.
indeed, it's better if it contains no idiosyncratic "voice" at all.


manuals also lend themselves to being "chopped up" and
farmed out to different individuals who'll then write sections.
that's not something you could do with a novel, or a treatise.
but for a manual, it's probably the very best thing you can do.


which is to say that i absolutely see the need for your system.


i just don't believe that it's going to work for books in general.
but that's probably not the argument that you are making, so
i don't mean to put it in your mouth, just find some agreement.


because then we can go on to something more productive, like
asking what kind of functionality we need for each type of book.


now, especially for the type of books that you are talking about,
tracking changes would seem to me to be one vital functionality.
so, i have made changes in all of the chapters here.  i don't know
if your change-tracking is in place, but if it is, it would be good if
you could show me the changes which have been made so far.


because what i expect you will find is that github does _not_ give
the kind of results that writers and editors want, and need, to get.
we need better tools for that, tools that are specialized for the type
of changes made by _writers,_ not by _coders._  and we're probably
going to have to code those tools ourselves, maybe from scratch.
