Where did the idea come from?

Well, Tomas Krag, a good friend of mine, once told me about this crazy idea of his. It was a pretty astonishing idea, nutty, naive and beautiful. He called his idea a Book Sprint and in the 5 minutes we had at an event in Budapest the idea went something like this:

In 2007 Tomas and a group of buddies, all very experienced and talented radio engineers and technicians, decided to write a free book about free wireless networks. The book was to be called "Wireless Networking in the Developing World". To write the book Tomas realised he had to collaboration was the only way to go. How, though, to collaborate on producing a book? What would this process look like and how would it work? 

Tomas' idea was simple - get everyone together in the same room and just work it out and that is essentially what they did. They had a friend in London with an apartment so they paid for them to go on a holiday for a week and then invaded their flat. Good deal for everyone involved.
